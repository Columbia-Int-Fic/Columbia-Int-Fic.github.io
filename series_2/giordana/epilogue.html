In the following weeks, Maura attempted many times to contact Liliana, but to no avail. Liliana refused to re-open this old wound of hers and blocked Maura for good, convinced it would keep her and her partner safe.

Liliana flew back to Milan with Rossana, this time paying for their own ticket. With the money that they had earned working at the nursing home, they were able to find a home. Rossana helped Liliana get her mental health back on track with slow, deliberate steps, starting with giving her space to do what she loved most: write poetry.

Maura remained in Palermo, puzzled and somewhat regretful of this entire anecdote. Nothing dramatic changed about her life, except for the fact that she now felt entitled to a healthy dose of selfishness and personal boundaries. She educated herself on codependency and made sure that her life did not rotate around solving her parents’ and her daughters’ problems.
